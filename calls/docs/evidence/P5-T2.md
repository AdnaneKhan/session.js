## Findings (relay path, deterministic in this environment across 4 runs)

WORKS over relay: TURN allocation + auth, ICE relay-pair nomination (both
sides' nominated pairs are `typ relay`; host/srflx candidates are gathered by
werift but never paired — forceTurn), DTLS handshake, SCTP data channel
(both directions — data channels opened).

DOES NOT work over relay (deterministic, both TURN server pairs):
1. **Callee-side sender readiness never opens.** Probed via the AudioBridge
   queue: caller's queue drains (connectionStateChange "connected" fired),
   callee's queue stays full forever (1 → 30 queued frames, never drained)
   even though the callee's data channel is open. werift's callee-side
   full-connectionState "connected" emission appears to be skipped under
   relay timing. (Loopback: both sides fine — offline-echo PASSes.)
2. **Caller→callee RTP never arrives** even after the caller's sender is
   ready and drained (≥30 SRTP packets written; callee's onReceiveRtp never
   fired), while SCTP flows both ways on the same relay path. Either a
   werift relay-RTP emission gap (TURN channel binding for RTP vs SCTP) or
   environmental UDP shaping that retransmitting SCTP survives and raw RTP
   does not — cannot be distinguished from this layer.

Classification: **media-layer delta** (media plane is read-only for this
wave) with possible environmental contribution. For the NETWORK lifecycle
test (policy "all") audio flows perfectly — 150/150 frames over the real
swarm (P4-T3.md) — so the production-default path is validated; relay-only
mode is documented as "hides host IPs but degraded/unsupported in v1 until
the media wave resolves the werift relay-RTP findings".
